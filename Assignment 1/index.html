Top 5 Tabletop Games

  1. Magic The Gathering

    Magic The Gathering is a card game where you are a planeswalker, a powerful wizard who can use the mana from the planes to summon creatures and cast spells. There are 5 colors in magic, each with their own strengths and weaknesses, but black is the best and blue is the worst.

  2. Draw Steel

    Draw Steel is a Tabletop RPG designed by Matt Colville, who previously has made several supplemental books for D&D 5th edition. Draw Steel tries to be a more tactical rpg than D&D, with more status conditions, forced movement, and more combat orriented abilites taking the focus.

  3. Root

    Root is an assymetric strategy game where you fight for control of the woodlands. In the base game there are 4 factions to choose from, each with a completely different playstyle from the others. The assymetric design is immediately obvious with the starting positions of each player, for example, Marquisse de Cat starts with all clearings except one under their control.

  4. Bargain Quest

    Bargain Quest is a game where you are a merchant selling items to adventurers to use on their quest. Your goal is to become the most prestigious shop in the land, so you must sell adventurers equipment that will help them finish their quest to earn you fame!

  5. Wingspan

    Wingspan is an engine building game where you collect birds of differing species to generate resources to earn points. Some birds lay eggs, some draw you cards, some hunt other birds from the deck to earn points. Everyone is competing for birds from the same deck, so trying to build a unique strategy will help prevent other players from disrupting your engine.
